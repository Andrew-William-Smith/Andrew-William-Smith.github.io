This is a summary of the formulas covered in my Honours Geometry class.  For each formula, I have given the formula itself, what it can help you calculate, and what values you must have to effectively use the formula.

### Midpoint formula
**Given:** Two colinear points\\
**Find:** The point equidistant from the two points (midpoint)\\
**Formula:** $$\left(\tfrac{x_1+x_2}{2},\ \tfrac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right)$$, where <span class="vardef">$$\left(x_1, y_1\right)$$ and $$\left(x_2, y_2\right)$$ are colinear points</span>.

### Slope formula
**Given:** Two colinear points\\
**Find:** The slope formed by the line through the two points\\
**Formula:** $$\tfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$$, where <span class="vardef">$$\left(x_1, y_1\right)$$ and $$\left(x_2, y_2\right)$$ are colinear points</span>.

### Point-slope form
**Given:** The slope of a line and a point on the line\\
**Find:** The equation of the line\\
**Formula:** $$y-y_1=m\left(x-x_1\right)$$, where <span class="vardef">$$\left(x_1, y_1\right)$$ is a point on a line</span> and <span class="vardef">$$m$$ = the slope of the line</span>.

### Slope-intercept form
**Given:** The slope of a line and its *y*-intercept\\
**Find:** The equation of the line\\
**Formula:** $$y=mx+b$$, where <span class="vardef">$$m$$ = the slope of the line</span> and <span class="vardef">$$b$$ = its *y*-intercept</span>.

### Distance formula
**Given:** Two colinear points\\
**Find:** The length of the segment between the two points\\
**Formula:** $$\sqrt{\left(x_2-x_1\right)^2+\left(y_2-y_1\right)^2}$$, where <span class="vardef">$$\left(x_1, y_1\right)$$ and $$\left(x_2, y_2\right)$$ are colinear points</span>.

### Sum of the measures of the interior angles of a polygon
**Given:** The number of sides of a polygon\\
**Find:** The sum of the measures of the interior angles of the polygon\\
**Formula:** $$\left(n-2\right)180^{\text{o}}$$, where <span class="vardef">$$n$$ = number of sides</span>.

### Measure of an external angle of a regular polygon
**Given:** The number of sides of a regular polygon\\
**Find:** The measure of an external angle of the polygon\\
**Formula:** $$\tfrac{360^{\text{o}}}{n}$$, where <span class="vardef">$$n$$ = number of sides</span>.

### Equation of a circle
**Given:** The center point of a circle and either a point on the circle or the circle's radius\\
**Find:** The equation of the circle, which can be used to find any of the variables below\\
**Formula:** $$\left(x-h\right)^2+\left(y-k\right)^2=r^2$$, where <span class="vardef">$$\left(x, y\right)$$ is a point on the circle</span>, <span class="vardef">$$\left(h, k\right)$$ is the center of the circle</span>, and <span class="vardef">$$r$$ = radius</span>.

### Pythagorean theorem
**Given:** The lengths of two sides of a *right* triangle\\
**Find:** The length of the third side\\
**Formula:** $$a^2+b^2=c^2$$, where <span class="vardef">$$a$$ and $$b$$ are the lengths of the legs</span> and <span class="vardef">$$c$$ is the length of the hypotenuse</span>.

### Similar solids
**Given:** Any combination of the surface area, volume, and area of two similar solids\\
**Find:** All of the other values\\
**Formula 1:** $$\left(\tfrac{S_1}{S_2}\right)^2=\tfrac{A_1}{A_2}$$, where <span class="vardef">$$S_1$$ and $$S_2$$ are surface areas</span> and <span class="vardef">$$A_1$$ and $$A_2$$ are areas</span>.\\
**Formula 2:** $$\left(\tfrac{S_1}{S_2}\right)^3=\tfrac{V_1}{V_2}$$, where <span class="vardef">$$S_1$$ and $$S_2$$ are surface areas</span> and <span class="vardef">$$V_1$$ and $$V_2$$ are volumes</span>.

### Chord-chord intersection theorem
<img src="http://www.mathwarehouse.com/geometry/circle/images/product-of-chords/product-chords-segments-picture.gif" width="200">\\
**Given:** A circle with two intersecting chords, the measures of three sections of which are given\\
**Find:** The remaining section\\
**Formula:** $$a\cdot b=c\cdot d$$, where <span class="vardef">$$a$$ and $$b$$ are the sections of one chord</span> and <span class="vardef">$$c$$ and $$d$$ are the sections of the other</span>.

### Secant-secant theorem
<img src="http://www.mathwarehouse.com/geometry/circle/images/secant-tangent-sides/secant-secant-picture-example.gif" width="200">\\
**Given:** A circle intersected by two secants with a common endpoint, the measures of three sections of which are given\\
**Find:** The remaining section\\
**Formula:** $$b\left(a+b\right)=d\left(c+d\right)$$, where <span class="vardef">$$a$$ and $$c$$ are sections of one secant inside the circle</span> and <span class="vardef">$$b$$ and $$d$$ are the sections outside the circle</span>.

### Tangent-tangent theorem
<img src="http://image.mathcaptain.com/cms/images/41/circle-tangent.PNG" width="200">\\
(Note: <span class="vardef">$$PQ$$ = $$a$$</span> and <span class="vardef">$$PR$$ = $$b$$</span>.)


**Given:** A circle intersected by two tangents with a common endpoint, the measure of one of which is given\\
**Find:** The measure of the other tangent\\
**Formula:** $$a=b$$, where <span class="vardef">$$a$$ and $$b$$ are the measures of the tangents</span>.

### Tangent-secant theorem
<img src="https://dr282zn36sxxg.cloudfront.net/datastreams/f-d%3Acda77f0f455edefadd01497f019b2b39c5e95ae1cf9fd36164785439%2BIMAGE%2BIMAGE.1" width="200">\\
**Given:** A circle intersected by a tangent and a secant with a common endpoint, with at least two measures given\\
**Find:** The other measure\\
**Formula:** $$a^2=b\left(b+c\right)$$, where <span class="vardef">$$a$$ is the measure of the tangent</span>, <span class="vardef">$$b$$ is the section of the secant outside the circle</span>, and <span class="vardef">$$c$$ is the section of the secant inside the circle</span>.

## Tangent-tangent arc
<img src="http://www.regentsprep.org/regents/math/geometry/gp15/circle7.gif" width="200">\\
**Given:** The major and minor arcs of a circle intersected by two tangents with a common endpoint\\
**Find:** The measure of the angle formed by the intersecting tangents\\
**Formula:** $$\text{m}\angle=\tfrac{1}{2}\left(j-n\right)$$, where <span class="vardef">$$j$$ = the measure of the major arc</span> and <span class="vardef">$$n$$ = the measure of the minor arc</span>.

## Chord-chord arc
<img src="http://www.regentsprep.org/regents/math/geometry/gp15/circle4.gif" width="200">\\
**Given:** The measures of the intercepted arcs of two chords\\
**Find:** The measure of angle formed by the intersection of the arcs\\
**Formula:** $$\text{m}\angle=\tfrac{1}{2}\left(a_1+a_2\right)$$, where <span class="vardef">$$a_1$$ and $$a_2$$ are the measures of the intercepted arcs of two chords</span>.

## Secant-secant arc
<img src="http://www.regentsprep.org/regents/math/geometry/gp15/circle10.gif" width="200">\\
**Given:** The measures of the intercepted arcs of two secants with a common endpoint\\
**Find:** The measure of the angle formed by the secants\\
**Formula:** $$\text{m}\angle=\tfrac{1}{2}\left(a_>-a_<\right)$$, where <span class="vardef">$$a_>$$ = the measure of the larger arc</span> and <span class="vardef">$$a_<$$ = the measure of the smaller arc</span>.
